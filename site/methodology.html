What this is

The Rest of the Record is a searchable microscope on the paper trail New York City and New York State agencies generate beyond the news cycle: adjudicative decisions, audits, complaints, penalty recommendations, oversight reports, and other public records that aren't already aggregated elsewhere. We pull from each source on a nightly schedule, normalize records into a single schema, and ship a static full-text index that runs entirely in your browser.

We don't host the original documents — every record links back to the official source.

What's not (yet) indexed

  • NYPD Trial Decisions Library (pre-2021) — at nypdonline.org. Pre-2021 NYPD trials lived under the Deputy Commissioner of Trials and aren't on Open Data; the portal is JS-rendered and requires headless browser scraping. (Post-2021 NYPD discipline now flows through OATH Trials Division and is indexed above.)
  • City Council hearing transcripts and testimony — the City Clerk paywalls these; we plan to index what citymeetings.nyc has.
  • Court dockets (Housing Court, eCourts WebCivil/WebCriminal) — out of scope. CourtListener-style coverage of NY state courts is a much larger project.
  • Landmarks Preservation Commission, Loft Board, BSA, ECB violations — partly covered by New York Law School's CityAdmin Online. We may add direct scrapers for the ones their index misses.
  • NYC Rules (rules.cityofnewyork.us) — proposed and adopted agency rules. Sitemap lists 621 rules; on the v1.3 list (Cloudflare protection requires extra handling).
  • NY State Comptroller audits of NYC agencies — sitemap-driven enumeration; on the v1.3 list.
  • City Council Legistar — meeting agendas, items, and transcripts. Has a public web API (citymeetings.nyc covers some, with AI segmentation).

How matching works

The browser-side index is built with MiniSearch. Searches run AND across terms, with prefix matching and light fuzzy matching. Title, agency, and respondent fields are boosted over body text. We don't yet support phrase queries with quotes — that's planned.

Data integrity

We don't modify or interpret the source records. Outcomes, penalties, and dates come straight from the upstream source — including their typos and inconsistencies. If a record looks wrong, follow the link and check the original. If the original is wrong, that's worth flagging to the agency that published it.

If you spot a bug in our scraping or normalization, please file an issue at github.com/joshgreenman1973/nyc-decisions.

Scrape ethics

All sources are public. We use a descriptive User-Agent string with contact info, cap concurrency at one request at a time, and prefer Socrata APIs over HTML scraping where available. We cache PDF downloads locally so we don't re-fetch unchanged documents.

The Rest of the Record is an independent civic-data project.
