NYC Decisions

A search index for New York City rulings, oversight reports, and administrative decisions.

Every day, New York City and New York State agencies issue hundreds of rulings, audits, dispositions, and oversight reports. Most are legally public — but they live in dozens of incompatible places: PDFs filed in dated folders, JavaScript-rendered portals, fee-walled City Clerk archives, and the occasional Open Data dataset. Even seasoned reporters routinely lose hours hunting for them.

NYC Decisions pulls from a growing set of those sources, normalizes the records into a common shape, and lets you search across all of them in one place. The current index covers both divisions of the Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings (OATH) — the Trials Division (which now houses NYPD disciplinary trials post-2021, plus Loft Board, TLC, and other agency adjudications) and the high-volume Hearings Division (summons court) — along with the Civilian Complaint Review Board (CCRB) complaints and penalty recommendations, the Conflicts of Interest Board (COIB), the Commission on Human Rights, the Department of Investigation (DOI), the NYC Comptroller, and the Public Advocate. We chose these because they are public-records workhorses — lots of consequential decisions, lots of newsworthy material — and because they are not already well-aggregated elsewhere. (New York Law School's CityAdmin Online handles a slice of administrative-law records, but no single tool indexes them alongside the oversight bodies above.)

Limitations to know before you cite anything you find here: OATH Hearings is capped at the last 180 days and 10,000 records; OATH Trials at the last 5 years; CCRB at the last 2 years; DOI, Comptroller, and Public Advocate currently include only what's linked from each agency's most-recent reports page (historical backfill is coming); we link out to the original document for every record and don't host PDFs ourselves; the OATH source data contains typos that we filter as best we can but can't fully repair. Several other bodies — the pre-2021 NYPD Deputy Commissioner of Trials archive, the Department of Health Board of Health, and City Council hearing testimony — are explicitly on the to-do list, not yet indexed.
